Vertex-Eval

Third-party trajectory-first agent evaluation — Pass^k as service-level contract, cross-channel evidence, HORIZON failure attribution, federated LaStraj corpus.

Design target: recover the 44 % of safety violations that trajectory-opaque evaluation misses (per Claw-Eval) by operationalizing cross-channel evidence + Pass^k + HORIZON attribution as a SaaS — with judge-human agreement approaching HORIZON's κ = 0.84.

What Vertex-Eval is

A SaaS evaluation platform that takes the methodology demonstrated in Claw-Eval (cross-channel evidence, Pass^k metric, fine-grained rubrics) and HORIZON (trajectory-grounded failure attribution) and packages it as a product. Customers push traces (+ audit logs + state snapshots) and get back trajectory-level scores, Pass^k metrics, failure-class distributions, and SLA breach alerts.

Niche

Teams shipping production agents who need honest, trajectory-first evaluation with consistency guarantees.

Not

A trace-storage product (LangSmith/Langfuse); a model provider; an agent runtime.

Status

Novel contributions

1. Pass^k as SLA contract

Claw-Eval introduced Pass^k as a metric; Vertex-Eval turns it into a product SLA customers can buy against. First productization.

2. Cross-channel evidence as third-party API

Trace + audit + snapshot triangulation, served as SaaS. Operationalizes Claw-Eval's discipline for teams who can't build it in-house.

3. Decorrelation metrics from the LLN thesis

Pairwise failure correlation across deployed instances — measures population-scale reliability per the chaos-engineering thesis.

4. LaStraj federation

Opt-in, anonymized, reviewer-vetted cross-tenant adversarial-trajectory corpus. Builds the community agent-security benchmark that doesn't exist today.

5. HORIZON attribution as default

Every eval run ships failure-class attribution with quoted evidence. Raises research methodology to operational signal.

Why this stack

Why Pass^k as SLA?

Pass@k is generous; customers deploying agents want consistency, not lucky runs. Pass^k aligns with real product experience.

Why cross-channel evidence?

Trajectory-opaque eval misses 44 % of safety violations per Claw-Eval. The whole product thesis is operationalizing the fix.

Why federated LaStraj?

Human-crafted adversarial trajectories evade model monitors at much higher rates than auto-generated ones. A cross-tenant corpus is the only way to cover the long tail of human creativity.

Why HORIZON attribution by default?

"Pass rate dropped" isn't actionable. "plan_divergence spiked after yesterday's harness change" is.
